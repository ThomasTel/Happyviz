An exploration of happiness

This website looks at differences in levels of happiness around the world, then focuses on the factors that make people happy using data from the World Happiness Report, an annual landmark survey of the state of global happiness.

You will have the opportunity to compare your level of happiness with that of your compatriots, and see whether you share values with them.*
Scroll down to find out !

But what makes people happy?

Family life, freedom, GDP, health, trust in government or even generosity impact people’s happiness at different levels. When we look at the importance of each factor on national happiness, we can see that each country has its unique idea, or perception, of what happiness is.
For instance Angolans (ranked 140th in the World Happiness Report) do not value Freedom as an important factor of happiness while the Norwegians (ranked 1st in overall happiness) believe that it is highly determinant for their level of happiness.

With which countries do you share your perception of happiness?

At the end of the journey


Look at this chart, it highlights the strong and consistent correlation between GDP per capita and Happiness Scores. Wealth is thus the main factor that can be said to directly impact happiness. In fact, a general rule can be inferred: for a country to see a 1 point increase in Happiness score, it needs to multiply its GDP per capita by 10.
