Joke aside, what does make people happy around the world? This website explores the impact of various factors on national happiness. And the results are full of surprises...

According to the World Happiness Report, all countries are not equal when it comes to happiness.

One can easily assume that happiness is directly correlated to wealth. So at the national level, one could believe that the happiness score of a country corresponds to its GDP rank at the international level.

Let’s Take France and Mexico. One is a developed country while the other is developing. A french person is on average 5 times richer than an average mexican citizen.

mettre image

Yet, Mexico is the 14th happiest country while France only ranks 29th! So we see that even though there is a clear correlation between GDP and happiness (see below), there are exceptions.

mettre image

So let’s focus on the importance of the perception of what makes you happy. Indeed, the factors that make people happy differ greatly from one country to another. Family life, freedom, GDP, health, trust in government or even generosity impact people’s happiness at different levels.

When we look at the importance of each factor on national happiness, we can see that each country has its unique idea of happiness. For instance Angolans (ranked 140th in the World Happiness Report) do not value Freedom as an important factor of happiness while the Norwegians (ranked 1st in happiness) believe that it is highly determinant for their level of happiness.
